About the Founder 

Saeed Anwar, a renowned Pakistani former cricket player, well known for his unmatched batting skill and graceful style as a left-hander was born on September 6, 1968, in Karachi and rapidly became one of the best batsmen for Pakistan following his first match in 1989. Respected for his perfect technique and regular scoring of runs, Anwar became known as a strong starting batsman. On the 21st of May in 1997, he showcased an amazing performance where he scored 194 runs in one game against India in Chennai. This amazing performance not only set new records for the highest personal score in One Day Internationals at that time but also strengthened his status as a standout batsman during his time. During his distinguished career, Anwar collected more than 8,000 runs in ODIs and was very important in Pakistan's cricket wins. His impressive leadership and respected personality have become a motivation for young cricketers globally. Although he stopped playing internationally in 2003, Anwar's lasting fame for his excellence secures his honoured place in the history of cricket in Pakistan.
